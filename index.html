Article No. 2Source: Ofgem

Securing Open Data in Energy: Triage in Data Best Practice Guidance

Reference: Ofgem consultation

Executive Summary

Ofgem proposes that open energy data should be treated as a managed cyber-physical infrastructure interface, rather than simply as a transparency measure. The key objective is to balance data openness for innovation with appropriate controls for cybersecurity and system resilience.

Background and Regulatory Context

Energy data must flow effectively to support engineering decisions, improve system visibility, and reduce the cost of the net-zero transition. Increasing cybersecurity threats and the wider use of AI tools mean that open-by-default assumptions need careful risk review.

Technical Risk Framework

Risk = Likelihood x Impact x Public Exposure
Public Exposure = f(Temporal Granularity, Geographical Granularity, Asset Specificity, Cross-linkability)

A dataset may become more sensitive when it is highly detailed, asset-specific, frequently updated, geographically precise, or linkable with other public sources.

Assessment of Triage Models

Centralised model

Improves consistency and security but may reduce efficiency and limit local network interpretation.

Hybrid model

Combines central standards with company-level operational context, giving the strongest balance of consistency, security, and flexibility.

Educational model

Lower-cost and capability-building, but may create inconsistent sector standards.

Conclusion

Energy network data should be opened through a risk-based triage process so open data supports smart grid innovation, net-zero delivery, and market development without avoidable cyber-physical vulnerabilities.

CEng angle

Use this case to discuss proportionate risk management, evidence-based decisions, data governance, operational context, and the public-interest balance between innovation and system security.

Article No. 1May 2026

Adani Green Energy commissions the world's largest single-location battery energy storage system of 3.37 GWh; strengthens reliable and clean power access

Reference: Adani Green Energy media release

Key Facts

A 3.37 GWh BESS at the Khavda renewable energy hub was commissioned in May 2026, claimed as the largest single-location deployment outside China.

Why Storage Matters

Curtailment

Battery storage can absorb surplus renewable generation during high-output periods.

Evening peak support

BESS can discharge during peak demand periods, shifting clean energy into high-value hours.

Frequency stability

In low-inertia systems, inverter-based BESS can provide fast active-power response.

Conclusion

Utility-scale battery energy storage systems should be assessed not only by installed energy capacity, but also by operational flexibility and grid-support capability.
