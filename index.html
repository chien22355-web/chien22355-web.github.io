Article No. 1May 30, 2026

Adani Green Energy commissions the world's largest single-location battery energy storage system of 3.37 GWh; strengthens reliable and clean power access

A review of why the 3.37 GWh Khavda BESS matters for curtailment management, evening peak support, frequency stability, dispatch control, and planning value.

Reference: Adani Green Energy media release

Key Facts

A 3.37 GWh BESS at the Khavda renewable energy hub was commissioned in May 2026, claimed as the largest single-location deployment outside China. The project adds 3.37 GWh of battery energy storage capacity; the associated MW rating depends on the storage duration, inverter rating, and grid-connection configuration.

Why Storage Matters

Curtailment

Battery storage can absorb surplus renewable generation during high-output periods, subject to state-of-charge limits and network constraints.

Evening peak support

BESS can discharge during peak demand periods, helping shift clean energy from high-generation hours to high-value consumption hours.

Frequency stability

In low-inertia systems, inverter-based BESS can provide fast active-power response to help arrest frequency deviation.

Control and Data Requirements

Effective operation depends on state-of-charge management, forecast-based dispatch, model predictive control, battery degradation-aware operation, telemetry, and grid-code compliance.

Economic and Planning Value

The planning case should consider avoided curtailment value, capacity value, ancillary service revenue such as frequency response and voltage support, network deferral, lifecycle cost, and degradation.

Conclusion

Utility-scale battery energy storage systems should be assessed not only by installed energy capacity, but also by operational flexibility and grid-support capability.
